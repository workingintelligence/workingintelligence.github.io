WHO WILL BE A URBAN FARMER?

A quick look at the history of farming, urban farming and microworking

15th Century

Machu Picchu, the first 'urban farm'

Machu Picchu which was built around the 15th century is one of the first documented urban farms. Machu Picchu was an Incan city built into the Peruvian mountains, using a form of stepped architecture the Incan’s were able to integrate farms directly into their city. This terraced land allowed the Incan’s to incorporate efficient forms of irrigation as well as optimize sun exposure for their crops.

The Invention of Future's contracts

Future contracts were developed in the 17th century which allowed farmers to secure a selling price of their crops and make this estimation at the beginning of their crop cycle. These contracts helped to mitigate risk and plan their year according to these early calculations. However, this strategy strips away a farmers chance of benefitting from higher price valuations because the contracts fix the price of their yield. In addition, farmers are require to pay fees to brokers and advisors in order to obtain these contracts adding additional overhead costs.

17th Century

2015

Rise of Micro-working

The Intuit 2020 report estimates that by 2020 more than 40% of workers will be independent workers — freelancers, contractors, etc. With advancements in technology, the ease of communication and collaboration has broken down the barrier for these form of working. In addition, new market places have been built which contributes to the growth of such a work force such as Uber, Mechanical Turk, Samasource, etc.

Peak Urban farming

Over the last decade vertical farming has undergone huge technological advancements and its viability as a agriculture production method has been rapidly increasing. These farms require less space and are highly efficient; furthermore, most of the farming process is automated in these devices. Because of the decrease in space requirements vertical farming also carries the affordance of fitting into urban spaces, warehouses, rooftops, even peoples homes.
